üåü What is LCM?

The Least Common Multiple (LCM) of two or more numbers is the smallest positive number that is divisible by all of them.

Think of it as the smallest number where all your numbers can "meet" when counting by their multiples!

Example 1: Finding LCM of 4 and 6

Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24...

Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18, 24...

LCM(4, 6) = 12 (the first common multiple)

Example 2: Finding LCM of 3, 5, and 10

Multiples of 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21, 24, 27, 30...

Multiples of 5: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30...

Multiples of 10: 10, 20, 30...

LCM(3, 5, 10) = 30
